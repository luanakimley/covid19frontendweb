Our battle with COVID-19 has been harsh and
it has impacted lots of people during these times.

Impact on Economy

Indonesia's household consumption, which sustained 60%
of it's economy has dropped from 5.02% to 2.84%. The weakening
economy worldwide had also made commodity prices lower and
made Indonesia's export to other countries stopped. The economic
turmoil happening due to COVID-19 can go down in history for the
Indonesians because it really impacted the management of state
finances with two revisions of the state budget and national
economic recovery efforts.

Impact on Employment

Like many other countries, Indonesia also experienced a significant
increase on the number of unemployed people. Before COVID-19, in
February 2020, 4.99% of Indonesia's people are unemployed, and in
March 2020, the percentage of unemployed people increased to 6.17%
to 6.65%. Indonesia's labour market is facing a crisis and currently,
the only sector that is going to grow is only the sectors related to
technology. The reason for that is because of the "work from home"
method that is now implemented in schools and offices.

Impact on Tourism

The tourism sector, which is one of the biggest sector contributing
to Indonesia's income, is the most affected sector because of COVID-19.
The spread of the virus has forced hotels, tourist attractions, malls, and
cafes to close for an unpredictable duration of time. Last year, 16 million
foreign tourists visited Indonesia, which gave an income of 20 billion USD,
but currently, there is no tourist at all. It is predicted that almost 15 million
people on this sector will lose their jobs until the end of the year.
